Overview of Green Technology

As China gradually adopts more sustainable practices, technology plays a crucial role in these efforts. From the widespread adoption of renewable energy to the popularization of electric vehicles and the construction of smart cities, green technology is driving China towards a more sustainable future.

This webpage will showcase China's efforts in sustainable development, with a particular focus on how technology can help achieve these goals. We will explore three key areas: renewable energy, electric vehicles and smart cities.

Explore the field of technology

Renewable energy

Solar energy

China is the world's largest producer and installer of solar panels. Through technological innovation, the cost of solar power generation has dropped significantly, making it one of the most competitive energy sources.

Learn more

Wind energy

China has the world's largest installed capacity of wind power. From onshore wind farms to offshore wind power projects, wind energy has become an important part of China's energy structure.

Learn more

Water energy

China has the largest installed capacity of hydropower in the world. Large hydropower stations such as the Three Gorges Dam not only provide clean energy but also contribute to flood control and shipping.

Learn more

Electric vehicle

The development of electric vehicles

China is the world's largest market for electric vehicles, with numerous domestic and international brands. Government policy support and technological innovation have driven the rapid development of electric vehicles.

Learn more

Charging infrastructure

China is building the world's largest electric vehicle charging network, including fast charging stations and battery swap stations, to address users' range anxiety.

Learn more

Innovation in battery technology

Chinese enterprises are at the forefront of global battery technology, constantly promoting the improvement of energy density, the acceleration of charging speed and the reduction of costs.

Learn more

Smart City

Intelligent transportation

Intelligent transportation systems optimize traffic flow through real-time data analysis, reduce congestion and emissions, and enhance urban traffic efficiency.

Learn more

Smart grid

Smart grid technology makes energy distribution more efficient, supports large-scale access to renewable energy, and provides real-time electricity consumption data.

Learn more

Environmental monitoring

The Internet of Things (iot) sensor network monitors air quality, water quality and noise levels in real time, providing data support for urban environmental management.

China's sustainable development timeline

The implementation of the Renewable Energy Law

China has promulgated the Renewable Energy Law, providing legal guarantees and policy support for the development of renewable energy.

2006

Electric vehicle promotion plan

The Chinese government has launched the "Ten Cities, Thousand Vehicles" project, promoting 1,000 new energy vehicles in each of the 10 cities.

2009

The signing of the Paris Agreement

China signed the Paris Agreement, committing to peaking carbon dioxide emissions around 2030.

2016

The goals of peaking carbon emissions and achieving carbon neutrality

China has announced its commitment to peaking carbon emissions before 2030 and achieving carbon neutrality before 2060.
